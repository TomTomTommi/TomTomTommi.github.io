Match-Stereo-Videos: Bidirectional Alignment for Consistent Dynamic Stereo Matching

Junpeng Jing, Ye Mao, Krystian Mikolajczyk
Imperial College London
Paper Code (Soon)

Abstract

Dynamic stereo matching is the task of estimating consistent disparities from dynamic stereo videos. Existing learning-based stereo matching methods typically prioritize optimal performance on a single stereo pair, treating stereo frames independently and resulting in temporal inconsistencies. Recent video methods apply cost aggregation in a sliding window across the time dimension, leading to low-frequency oscillations at the scale of the window size. Towards this challenge, we develop an alignment mechanism from bidirectional frames as a fundamental operation and further propose a novel framework, BiDAStereo, to achieve consistent dynamic stereo matching. Unlike existing works, we model this task as local matching and global aggregation. Locally, we develop a triple-frame correlation layer to pool information from adjacent frames to improve temporal consistency. Globally, to exploit the entire sequence's information and extract dynamic scene cues, we introduce a motion-propagation recurrent unit. Through this unit, global information can be recurrently propagated across the entire sequence. Extensive experiments demonstrate the outstanding performance of our method, showcasing improvements in prediction quality and achieving state-of-the-art results on various commonly used benchmarks.
